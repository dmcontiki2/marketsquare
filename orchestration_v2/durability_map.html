The strategy view of the self-healing system: what the app's AI should depend on to survive years and moves, and the loop that stops the same complaint from ever being fought twice.

The 5 steps to Nirvana 🧘 — toward the goal

1
Pin the substrate · rung 1 → 2 · an afternoonSet a pinned open-weight default in ai_provider.py; grep out any feature that still calls a vendor SDK directly. Removes model drift and starts killing ban/price risk.
2
Every fix leaves a guard · make Prevent mandatoryNo fix is "done" until it ships a class-level test into predeploy_check — the way evidence-true landed today. This is what stops complaint #100 from ever reaching a customer.
3
Reproduction on tap · close the amber keystoneA fixture/seed harness that replays any failing scenario on demand, so the Fixer verifies against reality instead of inferring. Kills the "fix it twice, fight it live three more times" battle.
4
Multi-host failover · rung 2 → 3The same open model served from ≥ 2 hosts, switchable by config flag. Un-bannable, price-stable, portable to any region at the drop of a hat.
5
Close the loop on your own box · rung 3 → 4 · NirvanaFlip the v2 conductor from SHADOW to LIVE on the green lane — then onto self-hosted weights on the Karoo. Detect → reproduce → fix → verify → guard → ship, unattended, on hardware you own and no one can switch off.
The top of the ladder and the last step of the loop are the same place: one box you control, that maintains itself, that nobody can switch off. Your instinct and your long-term vision were pointing at the same destination all along — this map just names it.
