TrustSquare — Filter & Search Engine

Design vision · broad strokes · 2 June 2026 · the maths kept under the hood

The whole idea in one line: a buyer writes a simple "shopping list" — what, how much, how trusted, how near — and one smart engine either shows the matches now (Search) or keeps the list and taps them on the shoulder when a match appears later (Wishlist). Same list, two doors.

Everything they see is close-first, trusted, and fresh — and nothing ever appears that isn't on their list. No ads barging in. No spam. The clever ordering happens invisibly; the buyer just sees a sensible, honest shelf.

One engine, two doors

The same "filter" powers both searching now and being notified later. Build it once.

A saved list is just a search you didn't run yet. Wishlist re-uses the email/notification rails you already have.

What the buyer actually sees

A tidy window: mostly close to home, a little from nearby, a little from the wider world — but every single card is on their list. When they reach the end, they choose whether to look further.

The ratios are a guide, not a cage. The key promise: out-of-filter results never sneak in — the buyer is always offered the choice to expand, clearly labelled.

How it decides the order (the bit we keep under the hood)

Behind the curtain, three simple dials decide which card sits where. You never see numbers — just a sensible order.

We tune the dials under the hood. The buyer just gets a list that feels right — closest & most trusted near the top.

The house rules — why this never becomes spam

You were firm: enhancement, not adverts. Here's the line that keeps it honest, drawn in pictures.

1 · Stays on your list — a boost can't show anything you didn't ask for 2 · A nudge, not a takeover — most of the screen is always earned, not paid 3 · Always labelled "Featured" 4 · Only trusted listings can be boosted — money can't lift junk

Where Tuppence fits (your idea, kept clean): a seller can spend Tuppence to reach the next suburb or two, or for a small rank nudge inside the buyer's filter — never cash, never noise, always capped and labelled. Because a boost can't leave the buyer's list, it's a help, not an advert. You already have the "reach the next city" plumbing (the listing_cities link) — this is the same idea, made a deliberate, fair choice.

Search and Wishlist — the same list, two uses

This is why we build it once.

  • Search runs the list right now and shows the window above.
  • Wishlist quietly keeps the list, watches new listings as sellers post them, and pings the buyer the moment a match lands — by email or push. (It re-uses the very notification pipeline we just switched on for introductions.)
  • One "list" + one "matcher" powers both. Zillow, eBay and Rightmove all work exactly this way: a saved search is an alert.

How we'd build it — four plain steps

Each step stands on its own and is useful the day it ships. Only the first is needed for launch.

Steps 2–4 roll out as supply grows — there's no rush until a city's listings get large.

Is this a tall order? No. The hard thinking is this page. The mechanics are standard marketplace fare, and you already own about half the parts:

  • the "reach the next city" link (listing_cities) — the surrounding-cities tier, half-built;
  • Trust scores on every listing — your ranking dial is already there;
  • the email + push pipeline we just switched on — that's your Wishlist alerts;
  • Tuppence — the fair, no-refund, no-ads currency for the boost.

The only piece truly needed before launch is Step 1 (make filters honest, so a busy city never hides matches). Everything else is a calm, staged rollout.
