# Field of the Invention
Networked marketplace systems; access control and metering of computational and human-mediated services; transactional database state machines; privacy-preserving identity disclosure protocols.
# Background
Existing marketplace platforms disclose seller identity and contact details either freely or upon registration. None require a buyer to make an irreversible commitment signal — surrendering a non-refundable unit of value — before any identifying information about the seller is disclosed; the result is a high volume of low-quality, uncommitted inquiries that burden sellers. Pay-to-contact platforms (e.g. Bark, Thumbtack) place the charge on the SELLER, transmitting buyer identity freely. Prior lead-marketplace systems (e.g. US20120030054A1, "Lead Marketplace System and Method with Ping Campaigns") likewise charge the SELLER / lead-buyer per delivered consumer contact and transmit consumer-identifying detail as the purchased asset — the inverse of the present buyer-pays-blind model. <!-- prior-art design-around: US20120030054A1 lead marketplace ping-campaign — seller-side charge, consumer identity is the sold asset; distinguished from buyer-paid-blind. # Summary of the Invention
A marketplace platform comprising: (i) a single-table unified participation model in which buyer/seller role is derived from transaction state rather than stored; (ii) a platform-native, non-transferable, cash-non-refundable value unit ("Tuppence") committed by atomic HOLD at service request, burned only on verified delivery, and released in full on non-delivery; (iii) an anonymous-introduction protocol in which no party-identifying information is transmitted before bilateral consent; (iv) a server-computed trust score, derived from an evidence-based signal-points catalogue offset by a time-decaying complaint-penalty subsystem (per C4), as the primary pre-commitment evaluation signal; <!-- alignment fix: Summary said "composite" — assembly note 5 + whitepaper §3.4 ground-truth audit confirm the fixed-weight composite was never implemented; aligned to the signal-points architecture C4 recites -->
 (v) subscription-tier listing-slot enforcement applied server-side at publish time; (vi) an external machine-inference services layer in which each value-metered invocation is gated by a registered-user existence check and metered by the same hold ledger (free $0-first onboarding assists being governed instead by the daily cost-ceiling pre-flight); and (vii) cost-governance instrumentation <!-- AUD-1-014 fix: (vi) narrowed to value-metered invocations, matching Claim A + whitepaper §3.7 --> wherein every external-inference call passes a daily cost-ceiling pre-flight and writes a spend-log record. The combination of (ii) and (vi) — one value unit, one hold state machine, two service classes with class-dependent settlement triggers — is the inventive spine.

# Claims (DRAFT — two-anchor structure per IP Brief v5 §6 / v6)
## Independent Claim A (Claim 7 family — integrated platform)
A marketplace platform system comprising a unified single-table participation model wherein user role is derived from transaction state; a plurality of subscription tiers whose listing-slot limits are enforced server-side at publish time by rejecting over-limit publication with a payment-required response, the number and pricing of tiers being implementation detail; a platform-native value unit that is buyer-paid, fixed-quantum, non-transferable between users, and non-refundable in cash; an anonymous-introduction subsystem disclosing party-identifying information only upon bilateral consent; and an external machine-inference services subsystem; CHARACTERISED IN THAT one and the same value-unit ledger gates both the human-introduction service and each external machine-inference invocation, each service request atomically committing a hold of a fixed quantum, the hold burning solely on verified delivery and releasing in full on non-delivery, and each value-metered external machine-inference invocation being conditioned on an indexed registered-user existence check executed before any external call, and every external machine-inference invocation — metered or free-assist — being conditioned on a daily cost-ceiling pre-flight, with every invocation writing a typed spend record, whereby external-inference cost is bounded and metered by the same technical mechanism that meters human introductions.
## Independent Claim B (Claim 2 — commitment-unit bundle)
A method of charging for marketplace introductions comprising requiring a BUYER to commit, prior to any disclosure of seller-identifying information and blind as to the seller’s identity, a fixed, non-negotiable quantum of a platform-native value unit that is non-transferable between users (no transfer endpoint existing) and non-refundable in cash under any circumstance; wherein the commitment is irrevocable at request time; the committed quantum burns conditionally and solely upon delivery of the introduction by counterparty acceptance; and upon non-delivery by counterparty decline or expiry of a bounded response window the committed quantum is released to the buyer’s balance, the release returning only the un-burned hold and comprising no restitution logic; the charge thereby being distinguished on every axis from a referral, finder’s or commission fee (buyer-paid; fixed; pre-introduction; outcome-independent as to commercial result; non-refundable; paid blind).
