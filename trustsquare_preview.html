How it works

1 · Browse anonymously. Buyers browse seller profiles with no names, phone numbers or emails visible — only each seller’s evidence-based Trust Score and their listing.
2 · Commit to connect. A buyer requests an introduction by committing 1 Tuppence (1T = USD $2), the platform’s non-transferable credit. It is held — burned only if the seller accepts, released in full if declined or expired.
3 · Meet on mutual consent. On acceptance, both parties receive each other’s contact details simultaneously. No spam, no cold calls, no advertising — commitment replaces noise.

Trust & privacy by architecture

Seller identities are protected until bilateral consent — a single, simultaneous disclosure event. Trust Scores (0–100) are computed server-side from verifiable evidence: credentials, permits, identity verification and platform history, offset by a time-decaying complaint penalty. No user can write their own score. Tuppence is a closed-loop, non-transferable service credit: not withdrawable as cash, no inter-user transfers. POPIA-conscious data handling; magic-link authentication (no passwords stored).
