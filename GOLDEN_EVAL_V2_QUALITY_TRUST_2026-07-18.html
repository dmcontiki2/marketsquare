4 · The agency message — seller knows, buyer doesn't

Today: SELL Step 6/6 shows the country legal must-haves + "accredited agencies manage what they may, and facilitate the rest". The buyer sees the anonymity block — and nothing about the legal side. Your instinct is right: the message is at least half for the buyer.
MOCKUP — buyer-side listing detail, collapsed strip under the trust block (platform voice, NOT in AI copy)
⚖️ THE LEGAL SIDE OF THIS PURCHASE · ZA · PROPERTY
1 · Offer to Purchase — binding once signed by both parties
2 · Conveyancing attorney — appointed by seller, paid by buyer
3 · Compliance certificates — electrical, gas, electric fence
Private sale — these steps are the parties' responsibility. Accredited agencies on TrustSquare manage what they may, and facilitate the rest with the right professionals.
On an agency-managed listing the last line flips to: "This listing is agency-managed — the steps above are carried by an accredited agency." — the agency's safety/legal/support advantage becomes visible buyer value, which is also the strongest argument for sellers to list through one.
Recommendation: yes, show the buyer — but structurally, never in the advert copy. Three reasons: (1) advert copy is the seller's item in the seller's (anonymous) voice — legal framing there reads as the seller giving legal advice, which is liability; (2) as a platform strip it is consistent, country-swappable (the legal-cards.js data already exists and ships per country), and can't be gamed or omitted by a seller; (3) it converts the agency pitch from seller-education into buyer-visible trust — completing the two-sided story you built the legal cards for. Reuse: same data source, same country gate (SF_LEGAL_LIVE, ZA-only until legal review), collapsed by default so it doesn't push the CTA down. Keep the AI prompts unchanged.
