TrustSquare — Global Marketplace Design

One unified design · global from day one · Filter · Wishlist · Tuppence-enhance · 2 June 2026 · maths kept under the hood

The one idea: a buyer writes a simple list — what · how much · how trusted · how near — and one engine serves it three ways: shows matches now (Search), watches for matches later (Wishlist), and lets a seller fairly stand out within that list (Tuppence-enhance). Same engine, three hats.

Why global isn't scary: your revenue is Tuppence + subscriptions only — you never touch the goods money (buyer and seller settle directly). So currency is a display concern, not a payments rebuild. "Global" = three buildable things: a worldwide geo-scope, currency display, and per-country onboarding. The geo-hierarchy you already have (Country → Region → City → Suburb) is the backbone.

Your three decisions, now baked in:
  • Nearby = within-country — the "nearby" tier pulls adjacent cities in the same country (one currency per tier). Cross-border adjacency can slot in later with no redesign.
  • Prices auto-convert to the buyer's currency, original shown alongside — and the price band normalises to their currency so it never lies.
  • Default breadth is category-decided — local-only things (services, viewings) default local; shippable things (collectibles) default wider.

You already run this 4-level hierarchy for ZA (9 provinces · 54 cities · 11,679 suburbs). Global = the same shape, more countries — adding one is config, not a rewrite.

Currency, the simple way (your choice): each listing keeps its native price; the buyer sees it converted to their own currency with the original alongside, and their price-band filter compares in their currency. The intro fee never wobbles — it's Tuppence, pegged to $2.

DeWalt drill  ·  R4,200  (≈ $230 · £180)  ·  intro fee: 1 Tuppence ($2, fixed)

Payment processors (Paystack for Africa, Stripe for the rest) only ever handle Tuppence top-ups + subscriptions, routed by country. The goods deal is buyer↔seller, off-platform — so no multi-currency checkout to build.

2One engine, three hats

The reason we design them together: it's literally the same filter + matcher underneath.

Build the list + matcher once. Search runs it live, Wishlist saves it, Enhance lets a seller nudge their place within it. None can ever leave the buyer's filter.

3What the buyer sees & controls

Close-first, every card on-filter, prices in their currency — and they hold the dials.

Scope Local · + Nearby · + Global (default decided by category; widens with subscription tier) Currency shown in yours, original alongside Hide global one-tap "local only"

The window is the one from the Filter brief: roughly local-heavy with a small nearby + global sprinkle, everything matching the filter, and an explicit "look wider?" step at the end — never a silent mix. Scope is also an entitlement: free sees local/nearby, the Global subscription unlocks the worldwide ring (a clean "unlock global?" prompt, not an ad).

4Buyer controls & stress-tests (the edge cases)

The behaviours that stop it becoming chaos — captured so they're not forgotten.

SituationWhat the engine does (plain English)
30 near-identical sellersFair rotation — whoever's been shown least lately surfaces first, reshuffled each visit. No one hogs every intro, no one's buried; all still appear. Small quality signals (photos, completeness) break ties.
Nothing local, plenty nearbyNo dead end: "nothing in your city — 8 nearby, 5 global," widen with a clear label, offer a Wishlist ("ping me when one's listed here"), and log it as a demand signal for where to recruit sellers.
Unrealistic filterHonest zero — never a fake match (a boost can't fill the gap). Then points at the one blocker: "nothing at R10 — 3 under R500" or "no seller rated ≥99; top near you is 92 — show those?"
Global subscriberWider rings unlocked; still close-first by default, with a richer global slice they can flip to "global-first" for rare items where distance doesn't matter.
"Don't show global"Scope control collapses to local (or local+nearby); sticky and category-smart.
Cross-country pricesAuto-converted to the buyer's currency; the price band normalises so it filters honestly everywhere.

The no-spam line (unchanged): an enhanced listing must still match the buyer's filter, is capped in volume, always labelled "Featured", and only trusted listings can be boosted. A boost is a relevant card nudged up — never an advert that barges in. Tuppence buys reach (to nearby cities, within-country) or a small rank nudge — never noise.

6Decisions

Adopted (yours): within-country "nearby" · auto-convert prices to the buyer's currency · category-decided default breadth.
Still open — small calls for later (none block the design):
  • FX rates — which source and how often to refresh (daily is plenty; display-only, so precision isn't critical).
  • Second market — which country after ZA (drives CityLauncher + processor priority). Design stays country-agnostic regardless.
  • Category → default-scope map — the exact list of "local-only" vs "shippable" categories.
  • Rotation window — how often the fair-rotation reshuffles ties.

Not a tall order — and global-first actually de-risks you. Because currency is display-only and the geo-hierarchy already exists, designing for the world now costs almost nothing extra and saves a painful rebuild later. You already own the parts:

  • the 4-level geo-hierarchy (built for ZA, generic for any country);
  • trust scores + the listing_cities reach link (ranking + the nearby tier, half-built);
  • the email + push pipeline (your Wishlist alerts) and Tuppence (the fair, currency-stable boost currency).

ZA can be the first market live (Stripe timing) — but nothing here is ZA-shaped. The only must-before-launch piece is Step 1; the rest is a calm rollout as cities fill.
