## 3. The Conversation

**Move 1 — Open with curiosity, not a number:**
> *"Thanks for listing — I'm genuinely interested. Before I make an offer, could you tell me the make, model, wattage, and roughly how many hours it's been played? I'd also love to see a few photos and maybe a short video clip of it powering up."*

*(Rationale: you don't know what you're buying. Getting the model narrows the comp range immediately and signals you're a serious, informed buyer.)*

---

**Move 2 — Make your opening offer with a reason:**
> *"I've had a look at similar amps on Gumtree and Cash Crusaders, and private-sale mid-range combos are moving in the R3,000–R4,000 band right now. Given that there are no photos yet and I'd be buying without any warranty, I'd like to offer R3,200 cash on collection today."*

*(Rationale: cite the market, cite the risk, and offer speed/cash as sweeteners.)*

---

**Move 3 — If the seller counters high (e.g., "Best I can do is R4,300"):**
> *"I appreciate that — I can stretch to R3,700 if you can confirm the brand and model and send me photos showing the speaker, back panel, and power-on. If it's everything you say it is, we can arrange collection this week."*

*(Rationale: splits the gap toward your target, ties acceptance to the information you need, and creates urgency without desperation. If the seller refuses photos, that's your exit signal — see Section 4.)*

## 4. Leverage & Timing

### ✅ Facts that strengthen your hand

1. **No photos posted.** At trusted retailers like Cash Converters, quality guitar amps are available at a fraction of the cost of new ones, and are carefully checked before being resold. A private seller with no photos cannot offer that assurance — the information asymmetry is yours to exploit as a price lever.

2. **Comparable supply is plentiful.** Gumtree's South African listings offer a large and varied collection of second-hand amplifiers. You have walk-away options readily available, which removes urgency from your side.

3. **No warranty on a private sale.** Cash Crusaders sells pre-owned guitar amps with a 6-month warranty — for prices often well below R4,500. The seller's R4,500 ask carries none of that protection, which is a genuine cost to you.

### 🚩 Red flags that should end the negotiation

1. **Zero photos after you've asked.** A seller who won't photograph a working amp is either hiding cosmetic damage, speaker damage, or doesn't have the item. Walk away immediately.

2. **Cannot confirm make/model.** A Zoom Fire 30 Vintage 35W amp in good condition was listed at R4,000; a Behringer BT108 fetches under R1,000 at Cash Crusaders. Without a model, you cannot know whether R4,500 represents fair value or a 300%+ markup. No model = no deal at this price.

3. **Refuses a live power-on demonstration.** Any legitimate seller of working electronics will let you test before paying. If they refuse or stall, assume a fault.
