## 1 · Area Snapshot

Centurion is conveniently located between Johannesburg (approximately 47 km away) and Pretoria (about 26 km away), with access to arterial routes including the R21, N1, and N14. Its position within the Tshwane Municipality makes it ideal for commuters travelling between Pretoria and Johannesburg.

The Centurion property market in 2025 is marked by steady growth, strong sectional title demand, and rising estate prices.

Rental increases have stayed modest (~4–5% year-on-year), with landlords prioritising occupancy over aggressive escalation.

### 🌐 Fibre Internet

Centurion has **excellent fibre coverage** — this is one of the area's strongest selling points for remote workers and professionals.

Fibre services are available in Centurion and surrounding areas through multiple providers, depending on the fibre network infrastructure installed in a specific suburb. Available networks include: Vumatel, Openserve, MetroFibre, Frogfoot, Evotel, Open Fibre, and DFA. ISPs actively serving Centurion include Mweb, Webafrica, Afrihost, Screamer Telecoms, and Vox.

When choosing a property, prioritise infrastructure readiness including fibre, security, and backup power. Always verify fibre availability at the specific street address before purchase, as coverage varies by suburb and even street.

---

### ⚡ Load Shedding / Utilities

Load shedding (Eskom-managed rolling blackouts) affects all of Centurion as part of the Tshwane Metro grid. LTE backup and UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply) options are recommended so load shedding doesn't interrupt connectivity; fibre for complexes, estates, and gated communities is widely available across Centurion. Many estate properties offer solar or generator backup — confirm this feature when viewing. Water and sanitation are managed by the City of Tshwane; service delivery is generally reliable in established suburbs but can be intermittent — verify at time of purchase.

### 🔒 Safety

Honest assessment — Centurion is **relatively safer than the Johannesburg average** but crime remains a reality in any South African urban area. Gated and boomed estates significantly reduce risk.

The Wierdabrug SAPS precinct (covering Centurion) recorded 4,229 crimes in April 2025–March 2026 — down 6.3% versus the prior year and 23.6% below its pre-COVID level.

In the Lyttelton policing area, contact crimes decreased 41.5% and property-related crimes declined by 16.6%, with residential burglaries dropping by 22.3%.

However, areas of concern remain: commercial crimes rose by 10.4%, and sexual offences increased in some precincts.

The Numbeo Crime Index for Centurion stands at 72.43 (Safety Index: 27.57), which is categorised as a high-crime perception rating — this is a **perception index** based on user surveys and should be weighed against the official SAPS downward trend data above.

**Practical advice:** Prioritise homes in **boomed, guarded, or fully enclosed estates**, which provide substantially better on-the-ground security. Areas like Amberfield, Midstream, Irene, and Eldoraigne (within estates) are widely regarded by residents as among the safer pockets.
