## 1. Verdict & Dream Spec — Can This Retirement Work?

**The short answer: Yes — with two hard constraints you must resolve before departure.**

Your profile is well-suited to Portugal's Algarve or Cascais coast. A healthy couple at 63, both with private pension + RA income, R3 million capital, and a clear lifestyle brief (walkable town, good healthcare, established expat community, safety) maps almost perfectly onto the D7 Passive Income Visa framework and the Algarve/Cascais property market.

### The 2 hardest constraints

**① Can your pension income clear the D7 couple threshold?**
A single applicant must show at least €10,440/year in passive income; with a spouse, add 50%, totalling **€15,660/year** (≈ R293,000/year or R24,400/month at today's rate). Your private pension + RA must demonstrably hit that number on bank statements. A common mistake is believing a large lump sum (your R3m) substitutes for recurring income — the Portuguese government prioritises regular, ongoing passive income over lump-sum amounts. Capital can reinforce an application but cannot replace verifiable monthly income. **If your monthly pension drawings fall short, restructure your RA drawdown before applying.**

**② Exchange control and capital transfer — plan well ahead.**
As of April 2026, South Africans can transfer up to R2 million per year via the Single Discretionary Allowance (no SARS approval required), and up to R12 million combined annually using both SDA and Foreign Investment Allowance. Your R3m capital therefore transfers in roughly two tranches across two tax years — workable, but requires timing. Start the SARS tax-emigration process early.

## 2. Visa & Legal — The D7 Passive Income Visa

> ⚠️ **RISK NOTE:** This section is informational only. Verify all thresholds and procedures with the Portuguese Consulate in Johannesburg (+27 11 484 3975) and/or a licensed Portuguese immigration practitioner before acting.

**Official source:** [AIMA (Agência para a Integração, Migrações e Asilo)](https://aima.gov.pt) — the authority that replaced SEF in 2023.

### What is the D7?
The D7 visa, also known as the Retirement Visa or Passive Income Visa, allows non-EU citizens to travel to Portugal and apply for a residence permit. It is ideal for financially independent individuals such as retirees who have reliable income from outside Portugal — from pensions, rental properties, dividends, or other investments.

### Income thresholds (2026)

| Applicant | Monthly minimum | Annual minimum |
|---|---|---|
| Main applicant | €920 | €11,040 |
| Spouse (add 50%) | +€460 | +€5,520 |
| **Couple total** | **€1,380** | **€16,560** |

You must prove a minimum passive income of €920 per month; the sum increases by 50% for a spouse. The minimum passive income requirement for the D7 Visa increases 50% for the spouse and 30% for each dependent child.

It is also required to maintain a balance in a Portuguese bank account equivalent to 12 months' income (€11,040 minimum), accessible after obtaining the residence permit card. This threshold increases by 50% if a spouse is included.

### What income qualifies?
Pensions are among the most widely accepted income forms. This includes both public and private retirement pensions, from a government, employer, or private pension fund. The D7 accepts income from pensions, rental income, dividends, royalties, interest, and annuities. Your private pension **and** retirement annuity both qualify.

### Application process
The process typically involves two steps: applying at the Portuguese consulate in your country of residence, then finalising residency status with AIMA (the authority that replaced SEF in 2023).

### Renewals & pathway to permanency
The D7 visa is valid for an initial 2 years, after which it can be renewed for an additional 3 years. After 5 years of legal residence, you can apply for permanent residency.

### Minimum stay requirement
There is a requirement to actually reside in Portugal to be able to renew a residence permit. The D7 minimum stay for visa holders is 16 months within two years.

### Spouse inclusion
If both spouses apply simultaneously at the Portuguese Consulate or VFS Global in South Africa, you can all relocate together immediately. However, if one partner moves first, under Law 61/2025 you will need to hold residency for two years before an adult dependent can legally join. **Apply together from Pretoria — this is the safest route for couples.**

### Pathway to citizenship
Portugal revised its nationality law in 2026, extending the general citizenship eligibility period from 5 years to 10 years for most applicants. This was signed into law by the President on 3 May 2026.

> R3,000,000 at €0.0535 = **≈ €160,500** (est.) | At a conservative €0.050 = **≈ €150,000** (est.)

### Pension portability — NOT a frozen-pension situation
South Africa has **no government/state pension** equivalent to the UK State Pension — so the UK "frozen pension" problem does **not** apply here. Your private pension and retirement annuity are portable to any country you choose to live in.

### Tax treaty position: South Africa ↔ Portugal
Portugal and South Africa share taxing rights on South African government pensions under the SA-PT double tax treaty. Unlike other major source countries, the SA-PT treaty allows South Africa to retain some taxing rights over pensions.

For **private pensions and retirement annuities** (your specific situation), Portugal generally taxes foreign pensions and social security income received by tax residents, although this depends on specific tax treaties.

The first €4,104 of pension income is tax-exempt in Portugal, but pensions and salaries must still be declared in the annual tax return. As Portuguese tax residents, you may be subject to progressive tax rates on Portuguese-sourced income (14.5% to 48%).

**Critical action:** Completing tax emigration from South Africa means SARS will only tax income earned in South Africa (e.g., rent from SA property you still own). All income earned or received in Portugal will be subject solely to Portuguese tax law, reducing your tax exposure and making managing your finances far easier.

**Get a cross-border tax specialist** (e.g., Sable International, FinGlobal, or a Portuguese tax accountant) to model your specific pension structure before departure. The SA-PT treaty is nuanced for private pensions.

### South African exchange control — moving your R3m

South African tax residents over 18 are eligible for: R2 million Single Discretionary Allowance (no SARS clearance required) + R10 million Foreign Investment Allowance (SARS tax clearance required).

**For your R3,000,000 capital:**
- Year 1: Transfer R2m via SDA (no approval needed)
- Year 2: Transfer remaining R1m via SDA
- Both tranches require a SARS tax compliance certificate and use of an authorised FX dealer

Financial emigration no longer exists. The updated process requires "tax emigration" with SARS. Once officially non-tax resident in South Africa and in good standing with SARS, you can transfer funds to an overseas bank account.

Under new SARB guidance, certain income categories (dividends, trust income, rental income) now require an Approval for International Transfer (AIT) PIN. Other forms of income such as interest, salaries, fees for services rendered, pensions, and annuities remain exempt. Your pension/RA income therefore transfers without AIT — but confirm this with your authorised dealer.

### Banking for new residents
Open a **Portuguese bank account before departure** (required for D7 application). Major banks serving expats: Millennium BCP, Novo Banco, Caixa Geral de Depósitos. International options: Wise, Revolut (for FX efficiency). You will need a Portuguese NIF (tax number) first — obtainable via the Portuguese Consulate in Pretoria or online with a fiscal representative.

## 4. The Home — Property Prices, Foreign Buyer Rules & MarketSquare Listings

### Foreign buyer rules — excellent news
There are no nationality restrictions on property ownership in Portugal. Foreign buyers only need a Portuguese tax number (NIF) and a bank account to complete the purchase.

### Purchase transaction costs
IMT (Property Transfer Tax): 0.3–7.5% depending on property value. Stamp Duty: 0.8% of the purchase price. Notary and registration fees: around €1,000–€1,500 in total. When you include all transaction costs like taxes, notary fees, and legal expenses, expect to add about 7% to 10% on top of the purchase price for a typical property in the Algarve in 2026.

> **Important 2026 rule change:** From 2026, Portugal plans a flat 7.5% IMT on most residential property purchases by non-residents. Buyers who become tax residents within two years of purchase are exempt from this flat rate. Since D7 holders become tax residents almost immediately, you should qualify for the progressive (lower) rate — confirm with your notary.

## 6. Healthcare — Quality, Hospitals & Medical Cover Reality

### System quality
According to Numbeo's 2025 Health Care Index, Portugal ranks 23rd globally and 14th in Europe for healthcare quality. With an average life expectancy of 81.58 years (2022), Portugal surpasses the EU average, underscoring the effectiveness of its healthcare system.

### Public SNS — your entitlement as D7 residents
Once you become a Portuguese resident under the D7 Retirement Visa, you gain access to Portugal's national health system (SNS). Services are heavily subsidised. Consultations typically cost €5–€10, basic ER visits around €15–€20, and many services (like chronic disease treatment) are free.

Your **blood-pressure medication** will be substantially subsidised — prescription medications are partially subsidised, with patients paying 10–85% of cost depending on the medication's importance. Routine hypertension medication is typically at the lower end of that range.

| **Hospital de Faro** | Faro | Public SNS; main regional hospital |
| **Hospital de Lagos** | Lagos | Public SNS + private options |

| **Lusíadas Albufeira** | Albufeira | Private group; [lusiadas.pt](https://www.lusiadas.pt) |

Among the best-known hospital groups are CUF, Hospital da Luz, and Lusíadas, all offering extensive networks across the country including the Algarve and Madeira. Popular international insurers include Allianz Care, Bupa Global, and Cigna, while Portuguese options include Fidelidade, Médis, Ageas Seguros, and Medicare.

### Private health insurance reality for a couple aged 63
A couple in their 60s in the Algarve pays around **€270/month** with Médis, including both inpatient and outpatient care, plus dental. Most insurers do not cover pre-existing conditions unless specifically noted (MGEN is one exception).

**Your blood-pressure condition:** Portuguese insurers handle stable, managed hypertension differently — some cover it with a loading premium, some exclude it, some (like MGEN) accept it. Obtain quotes from multiple providers (Médis, Multicare/Fidelidade, MGEN, Allianz) **before** finalising your move.

If you're applying for a D7 visa, you'll need private health insurance in place to prove you're covered when applying.

### What South African medical aid does NOT do abroad
Your South African medical aid scheme (Discovery, Momentum, Bonitas, etc.) provides **emergency-only cover abroad** for a limited period (typically 90 days). It will **not** cover you as a Portuguese resident. You must take out Portuguese or international private health insurance. Cancel your SA medical aid once you are resident — it is money wasted.

## 8. Rules & Differences

### Driving licence exchange — South African licence
South Africa is **not** an OECD or CPLP member, meaning your SA licence does not benefit from the simplified exchange route. If the licence was issued by a country not party to international conventions, you must exchange it immediately upon becoming resident.

**What you need to do:**
1. Obtain a "Confirmation Letter" or "Driving Licence Verification" from South Africa's Road Traffic Management Corporation (RTMC) or the relevant licensing department, which may need to be apostilled.
2. Submit to IMT (Instituto da Mobilidade e dos Transportes) with your residence permit, NIF, and a medical certificate.
3. Whether you need to take driving tests depends on whether Portugal has a bilateral agreement with your country. If so, you can usually exchange without exams; if not, both theoretical and practical tests are required.
4. Theoretical exams can be taken in English upon request.
5. Official IMT portal: [www2.gov.pt](https://www2.gov.pt/en/servicos/trocar-carta-de-conducao-estrangeira-por-portuguesa)

**Confirm with the South African Embassy in Lisbon** ([www.embaixada-africadosul.pt](https://www.embaixada-africadosul.pt)) whether a specific bilateral agreement exists — this determines whether you need tests. Engage a relocation service to handle the IMT paperwork.

### Tax residency
To qualify as a tax resident, an individual must spend more than 183 days in Portugal during the calendar year. Days do not need to be consecutive. As D7 holders, you **will** be Portuguese tax residents — file annually with the Portuguese Tax Authority (Autoridade Tributária).

### Metrication
Portugal is fully metric — same as South Africa. Speed limits in km/h, distances in km, temperatures in Celsius. No adjustment needed.

### Utilities quirks — **no load-shedding**
This is one of the most welcome differences from South Africa. Portugal has a reliable national electricity grid (connected to the European grid), strong investment in solar and wind energy, and **zero load-shedding**. During the first half of 2025, electricity cost €0.159/kWh (below the EU average of €0.208/kWh). Note: many properties don't have central heating and rely on space heaters or air conditioning, so during extreme weather your utility bill may double or triple.

### Other must-knows
- Obtain your **NIF** (Número de Identificação Fiscal) first — everything depends on it
- Register with a local **Centro de Saúde** (health centre) for SNS access immediately upon arrival
- **AIMA appointment times** for residence permit finalisation can be long — book as soon as you arrive
- Portugal drives on the **right** (opposite to South Africa's left — re-learning is required; this is non-trivial)
- Emergency number: **112** (police, ambulance, fire)

## Safety Awareness

### Overall verdict
In the 2025 Global Peace Index, Portugal ranks as the **seventh safest country in the world** and the fifth safest country in Europe, trailing only Iceland, Ireland, New Zealand, Austria, Switzerland, and Singapore.

### Statistics
According to Portugal's Annual Internal Security Report (RASI), general crime fell by 4.6% in 2024. The rate of robbery in public spaces reduced by 0.3%, and robberies at gas stations by 12.3%.

The Algarve recorded 26,666 crime reports in 2024, which was 478 fewer than the previous year — a decrease of 1.8%. The Faro district ranked fourth nationally for total crime reports, which reflects the millions of tourists who pass through each summer.

Portugal's homicide rate is approximately 0.7 per 100,000 inhabitants. Gun violence is effectively non-existent in Portugal.

### Area-specific picture
- **Violent crime against residents: extremely rare.** Violent crime, muggings and armed robbery are rare and not statistically concentrated in expat-frequented districts.
- **Petty crime: real but manageable.** As a major centre of international tourism, the Algarve has been noted for drug trafficking cases; pickpockets and other petty criminals exist in moderate numbers, particularly in Faro.
- Car break-ins at unguarded car parks near coastal beach areas in the Algarve do occur. Never leave valuables in your car.
- **Seasonal spike:** Seasonal spikes in theft and disorder are more common in summer months in busy Algarve resort towns. Choose year-round residential towns (Tavira, Loulé, Lagos) rather than peak-tourist resort centres.
- According to Gallup's 2024 Global Safety Report, 83% of people in Portugal felt safe walking alone at night.
