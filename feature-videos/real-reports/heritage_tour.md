## 1 · Route Overview

### Route Structure

Given that the distance from Kruger National Park to Cape Town is approximately 1,900–2,000 km depending on the route taken, and the most common road route involves travelling through Mpumalanga and Gauteng, then westward through the Free State and Northern Cape — a journey of around 18–20 hours of driving without stops, a 7-day round trip requires smart staging. The recommended approach is to drive north-east over two days (with an overnight in the Johannesburg/Gauteng area), spend two full days in and around Graskop and the Panorama Route, then two nights inside Kruger, before returning via the same N1 corridor — allowing a final overnight near Johannesburg before Cape Town Day 1.

**⚠️ IMPORTANT BUDGET NOTE:** A round-trip self-drive of ~4,000 km on a R25,000 budget for 2 adults is extremely tight once fuel, tolls, Kruger conservation fees, accommodation and meals are included. The cost estimate in Section 7 is fully transparent on this. Travellers should treat this as a floor budget and consider every cost-saving tip listed.

## 2 · The Sites & Their Story

### 🏛️ Site 1: Graskop & the Panorama Route (Mpumalanga Escarpment)

Graskop is a small town in Mpumalanga province, South Africa. It was established in the 1880s as a gold mining camp but now serves as a tourist destination and the timber industry. Rock paintings and other archaeological sites in the Graskop area indicate that people have lived here for thousands of years. Evidence from an archaeological site 40 km from the town has been dated to show hunter-gatherer occupation 27,000 years ago. Early ethnographical studies indicate that the wider Graskop area was inhabited by Eastern Sotho groups — the Pulana, Kutswe, and Pai — and the Tsonga people since before the 19th century.

In the 1870s, Abel Erasmus, Native Commissioner of the Transvaal Republic, owned the farm of Graskop. Following the discovery of gold at Mac Mac, the farm was bought by the Government of the Republic of Transvaal and settlers set up a mining camp. Pilgrim's Rest however remained a more popular mining site, so there was not extensive mining at Graskop in the 19th century. After the gold boom declined, Graskop shifted its focus to tourism, capitalising on its proximity to attractions like the Blyde River Canyon and God's Window — today a quaint mountain village offering a serene escape for nature lovers and adventurers.

It sits on the 'Panorama Route', which winds through some of South Africa's most beautiful landscapes in Mpumalanga province. There are various scenic viewing points over the dramatic Northern Drakensberg mountain range — the highest and longest in South Africa — and the Panorama Route also boasts the largest number of waterfalls in South Africa. Just 16 km away, the historic mining town of Pilgrim's Rest has been declared a National Monument, Provincial Heritage Site, and was added to the UNESCO World Heritage tentative list in 2004.

**Nearby natural heritage: Blyde River Canyon**
The Blyde River Canyon is the largest green canyon in the world at 25 km long, with sheer walls averaging 750 metres. The view of the Three Rondavels — a trio of curious pinnacles shaped like traditional African huts — and down to the dam below is as iconic as that of Table Mountain in Cape Town.

### 🏛️ Site 2: Kruger National Park (Limpopo & Mpumalanga)

Kruger National Park covers an area of 19,623 km² in the provinces of Limpopo and Mpumalanga in South Africa's northeast. It extends 360 km from north to south and 65 km from east to west. Areas of the park were first protected by the government of the South African Republic in 1898, and it became South Africa's first national park in 1926. It forms part of the Kruger-to-Canyons Biosphere Reserve, recognised by UNESCO. The park is managed by SANParks and is the flagship of South African conservation.

Long before the National Parks Act merged the Sabie and Shingwedzi reserves into Kruger, these plains were shaped by many lives. Early Stone Age hunters roamed the region over half a million years ago, followed later by San communities who left behind exquisite rock paintings — more than a hundred of these sacred sites still lie hidden across the park. The park's cultural heritage includes over 300 archaeological sites, including San rock art and Iron Age ruins at Thulamela, revealing human history dating back 500,000 years to Homo erectus.

The park is home to 336 tree species, 49 fish, 34 amphibians, 114 reptiles, 507 birds, and 147 mammals, and leads in environmental management techniques. In 2002, Kruger joined Zimbabwe's Gonarezhou and Mozambique's Limpopo parks to form the Great Limpopo Transfrontier Park — a 35,000 km² wildlife corridor for free-roaming animals. Cultural heritage sites currently open to the public include the Albasini Ruins, Masorini Ruins, and Thulamela.

## 3 · Safety Awareness

> ⚠️ *This is general awareness, not a security assessment or guarantee — check current local advisories before departure.*

**Daylight driving:**
Plan your trip ahead and include regular breaks every 2 hours or 200 km to avoid fatigue. Drive during the day for added safety. Buckle up, stay within the speed limit, and avoid overtaking on barrier lines. Factor in rest stops and refuelling stops; avoid driving at night, especially in unfamiliar territory.

**Roadworks and specific stretches:**
The N1 North between Kroonstad and Heuningspruit in the Free State has active roadworks for 24 km, expected until November 2026. Reduce speed and follow signage through this section.

**Panorama Route conditions:**
While roads are largely wide and well-maintained, there are occasional potholes along the Panorama Route which can be hard to spot at night and during fog and rain. Do not drive the R533/R534/R535 mountain passes after dark.

**Malaria:**
Kruger National Park falls within a malaria endemic area of South Africa. Consult your travel doctor/general practitioner about malaria prevention before visiting. Use repellent, sleep under a net or in screened accommodation, and wear long sleeves at dusk. There are resident medical doctors permanently based at Skukuza, contactable on +27 13 735 5638.

**Vehicle security:** Never leave valuables visible in your vehicle at viewpoints or rest stops. Lock your car at all times when parked at roadside stops on the N1 corridor. Use only official, well-lit fuel stations — Engen, Shell, and BP stations are common along the N1 and N4.

**Fuel and rest stops:** Vehicle fuel is available in several parks and in the main rest camps in Kruger and Kgalagadi. Skukuza includes a fuel station. Fill up at every opportunity through the Karoo — gaps between stations can exceed 150 km. Carry extra water.

**Load-shedding:** South Africa continues to experience intermittent load-shedding. Confirm that chosen guesthouses have generator backup. Major SANParks rest camps have their own power systems, though services may be reduced. Check [Eskom Se Push](https://eskomse.push.com) for the load-shedding schedule.

**Inside Kruger:** Never exit your vehicle except at designated picnic sites, rest camps, and specific stop points. Entrance and rest camp gates close at night; latecomers at rest camps will be fined.

**South Africa Emergency Numbers:**
- 📞 **112** — Emergency (mobile, works without signal on most networks)
- 📞 **10111** — South African Police Service (SAPS)
- 📞 **10177** — National Ambulance / EMS
- 📞 **+27 13 735 5638** — Skukuza medical doctors (Kruger)
