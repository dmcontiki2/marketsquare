**CRITICAL FLAGS before selling anything:**
- The first official coinage of the Union of South Africa was issued in 1923 and included silver coins: the 2½ shilling (half-crown), 2 shilling (florin), 1 shilling, 6 pence, and 3 pence. Any such coins in the box are silver and must be separated from base metal before pricing.
- The "Word of God" 40c RSA stamp (1987) was withdrawn and is highly collectible — check the Republic albums carefully for it.
- The Krugerrand, introduced in 1967, is the world's first one-ounce gold coin; if any are present in the coin box, they are in an entirely different league and must be valued against live gold spot.

## 2. Sell Strategy

### Lot-Split Logic
| Tier | Strategy | Reasoning |
|------|----------|-----------|
| T1 — Key silver/gold coins | **Sell individually** | Each coin's value depends heavily on date, grade, and silver/gold content. Bundling destroys upside. |
| T2 — Union stamp albums | **Sell as complete albums first; break only if unsold after 8 weeks** | Collector appeal of an intact, period album is greater than the sum of parts at this tier. |
| T3 — Republic stamp albums | **Sell as albums; break out only the "Word of God" 40c and any other scarce singles** | RSA albums sell well on Bob Shop/MarketSquare to local collectors. |
| T4 — FDCs | **Sell as a curated set; offer individual breakout only for pre-1960 covers** | Sets move faster than singles in this category; individual modern FDCs are a slow sell. |
| T5 — Loose stamps | **Bundle into a single "collector's lot"** | Not worth individual listing time at R1–R5 per stamp. One bulk lot listing. |
| T6 — Base metal coins | **Bundle as a single "junk lot"** | No individual collector demand for common base-metal coins. |

## 5. Watch-Outs

### 🔎 Authentication / Grading Worth Paying For

| Item | Grading Service | Est. Cost | Expected Uplift | Worth It? |
|------|----------------|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Any Union silver coin in apparent Extremely Fine (EF) or better condition | SANGS (South African Numismatic Grading Service, Randburg) or NGC | SANGS: enquire directly; NGC standard grading: ~$40/coin (coins up to $3,000 value); economy service ~$23 for pre-1990 non-gold coins + ~$28–$150 shipping | Can double or triple value at auction; uncirculated Union coins can command $500–$1,000 vs. $10–$50 ungraded | **YES — for any coin appearing EF or better** |
| 1931 Union shilling (rare date) | NGC or SANGS | As above | The 1931 Union silver shilling is one of the rarest and most valuable of the period, with small populations commanding significant premiums. | **YES** |
| Any possible Krugerrand or gold coin | City Coins / South Cape Coins free appraisal first | City Coins assesses authenticity, grade, and marketability; for bullion, they align to spot price + premium, giving a clear range with selling options. Free initial assessment | Gold Krugerrand: value = live gold spot; Krugerrands are VAT-exempt as legal-tender bullion in South Africa. | **YES — always get a bullion appraisal first** |
| Union of South Africa Official stamps (OFFICIAL/OFFISIEEL overprints) | Expert philatelic opinion (contact PFSA or Stephan Welz philatelic dept.) | R300–R800 for a written opinion | Individual SG-listed Official stamps can reach up to £6,000 | **YES — if Officials are present in the album** |
| Mass of common Republic definitives | None | — | Negligible | **NO** |
| Common circulated base-metal coins | None | — | Negligible | **NO** |

> Sending a rare coin to be officially graded by PCGS or NGC can be costly and is not really worth doing unless you are certain the coin is genuine and valuable. Get a free preliminary assessment from a local specialist (City Coins, South Cape Coins) before spending on international grading.

### ⚠️ Items NOT Worth Selling Individually
- **Common used RSA definitives (1961–1990s):** R1–R5 each. Time cost of listing exceeds return. Bundle into Lot E.
- **Post-1965 base-metal circulation coins:** No silver, no numismatic premium unless specific rare date. Bundle into Lot F.
- **Duplicate FDCs of the same issue:** Bundle; don't list separately.
- **Torn, heavily soiled, or creased stamps:** These are space-fillers only; include in bulk lot, do not list individually and describe honestly.
- **Modern Republic stamps (post-2000, used):** Near-zero collector demand in the secondary market.

### 🚨 Market Reality Check
- Prices for South African coins are orientation values drawn from public market data and recent sales; actual value depends on grade, eye appeal, certification status, and current demand.
- A rare Mandela coin is often only valuable when it is a proof coin sold by the SA Mint — do not assume any commemorative R5 in the box is valuable without checking the specific date and finish.
- Be careful: many scammers and fake antique coins are on the market. If approached by an unsolicited buyer offering cash for coins, decline and use verified channels only.
- Philatelic FDC market in South Africa is soft for post-1980 issues. Stephan Welz & Co acknowledges the stamp market has changed, but has re-established philatelic auction expertise and now offers online timed auctions.
