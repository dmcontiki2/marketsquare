## 1. Exam Profile

### 🔢 Mathematics

Grade 12 Mathematics is examined in **two separate papers**, both written in the same exam sitting period but on different days.

**Each paper is 3 hours long for 150 marks.** All candidates write two external papers as prescribed.

**Paper 1 — 150 marks | 3 hours** *(Topics from DBE Examination Guidelines)*

Every CAPS NSC Mathematics Paper 1 is built from the same six topic areas, with the same mark split. This is documented in the DBE Mathematics Examination Guidelines and has not changed for years.

| Topic | Approx. Marks |
|---|---|
| Algebra & Equations (incl. Inequalities & Nature of Roots) | ±25 |
| Patterns, Sequences & Series | ±25 |
| Functions & Graphs | ±35 |
| Differential Calculus | ±35 |
| Finance, Growth & Decay | ±15 |
| Probability | ±15 |
| **Total** | **150** |

Functions & Graphs and Differential Calculus together make up 70 marks out of 150, close to half the paper. If your prep time is limited, those two are where you bank the highest return per hour of study.

**Paper 2 — 150 marks | 3 hours** *(Topics from DBE Examination Guidelines)*

Paper 2 covers Analytical Geometry, Trigonometry, Euclidean Geometry and Statistics.

| Topic | Approx. Marks |
|---|---|
| Euclidean Geometry (Theorems & Proofs) | ±50 |
| Trigonometry | ±40 |
| Analytical Geometry | ±40 |
| Statistics | ±20 |
| **Total** | **150** |

Euclidean geometry, analytical geometry, and trigonometry together carry 120 marks out of 150. Geometry proofs in particular are a significant mark source.

> **⚠️ Cognitive Levels Note:** Each paper is 3 hours long for 150 marks. That works out to 1.2 minutes per mark as a rough guide. The number in brackets after each question tells you exactly how many steps the marker is looking for. A (4) next to a calculus question means four marks are allocated — that means four distinct steps in your working. If you write two lines and get an answer, you have almost certainly missed two marks.

> **📅 November 2026 Exam Dates:** The October/November 2026 NSC timetable is published by the DBE. Mathematics Paper 2 falls on Monday, 1 November (Week 2 of exams). Mathematics Paper 1 falls in Week 1 (around 26–30 October — **confirm exact date** at [education.gov.za](https://www.education.gov.za/Curriculum/NationalSeniorCertificate(NSC)Examinations/ExamTimetable.aspx)). The full October/November timetable PDF is available from the WCED at [this link](https://www.westerncape.gov.za/education/files/wcg-blob-files?file=2026-03%2Foct-nov-2026-nsc-timetable-final-february-2026.pdf&type=file).

### 🔬 Physical Sciences

South Africa's Matric Physical Sciences examinations test both conceptual understanding and problem-solving ability across Physics and Chemistry topics, including mechanics, electricity, chemical change and organic chemistry.

The DBE Examination Guidelines document Assessment in Grade 12 — including Format of question papers, Weighting of cognitive levels, and Weighting of prescribed content — with Paper 1 covering Physics and Paper 2 covering Chemistry.

**Paper 1 — Physics — 150 marks | 3 hours**

Paper 1 covers: Mechanics; Waves, Sound and Light; Electricity and Magnetism; Matter and Materials.

| Topic | Approx. Marks |
|---|---|
| Mechanics (Newton's Laws, Momentum, Work-Energy, Projectile Motion) | ±65 |
| Waves, Sound & Light (Doppler, EM spectrum) | ±17 |
| Electricity & Magnetism (Circuits, Electrodynamics) | ±53 |
| Matter & Materials (Optical phenomena, atomic nuclei) | ±15 |
| **Total** | **150** |

**Paper 2 — Chemistry — 150 marks | 3 hours**

Under the CAPS curriculum, Physical Sciences Paper 2 covers Chemistry topics.

| Topic | Approx. Marks |
|---|---|
| Organic Chemistry (IUPAC, reactions, macromolecules) | ±56 |
| Chemical Equilibrium (Le Chatelier's Principle, Kc) | ±24 |
| Acids & Bases (pH, reactions, titrations) | ±28 |
| Electrochemistry (Redox, galvanic/electrolytic cells) | ±26 |
| Chemical Industry | ±16 |
| **Total** | **150** |

> **⚠️ Marking Rules:** Marks can only be allocated for substitutions when values are substituted into formulae. If a final answer to a calculation is correct, full marks will not automatically be awarded. Markers will always ensure that the correct/appropriate formula is used and that workings are shown.

> **📅 November 2026 Exam Dates:** Physical Sciences Paper 1 and Paper 2 are typically scheduled in Weeks 1–2 of the November sitting. **Verify exact dates** at [education.gov.za – Exam Timetable](https://www.education.gov.za/Curriculum/NationalSeniorCertificate(NSC)Examinations/ExamTimetable.aspx). ⚠️ *The November 2026 individual subject dates were not yet fully extractable from the published source at time of writing — confirm with your school or directly at the DBE website.*

## 2. Diagnostic — Self-Check Questions (Week 1)

Complete these before starting the plan. Mark honestly. Any question where you are unsure = a weak area → flag it → front-load that topic in your weekly sessions.

### 🔢 Mathematics Diagnostics

*(Mapped to official CAPS topic list)*

| # | Question | Topic Tested |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Solve: 2x² − 5x − 3 = 0 by factorisation AND by the quadratic formula. State the nature of the roots using the discriminant. | Algebra & Equations / Nature of Roots |
| 2 | The 3rd term of a geometric series is 18 and the 6th term is 486. Find the common ratio *r* and the sum of the first 10 terms. | Patterns, Sequences & Series |
| 3 | Sketch f(x) = −2(x+1)² + 8 and g(x) = 2ˣ − 4 on the same set of axes. Label ALL intercepts and turning points/asymptotes. Find the values of x for which f(x) > g(x). | Functions & Graphs |
| 4 | Given h(x) = x³ − 6x² + 9x, find h'(x), determine the coordinates of the turning points, and sketch the curve. | Differential Calculus |
| 5 | R15 000 is invested at 9% p.a. compounded monthly for 3 years, then withdrawn and reinvested at 10% p.a. simple interest for 2 years. Find the final amount. | Finance, Growth & Decay |
| 6 | A bag contains 5 red and 3 blue marbles. Two are drawn without replacement. Calculate P(both red). Draw a tree diagram. | Probability |
| 7 | Prove that the angle subtended by a chord at the centre is twice the angle subtended at the circumference. | Euclidean Geometry (Paper 2) |
| 8 | Determine the equation of the circle with centre (−2; 3) passing through (1; −1). Find the equation of the tangent to the circle at (1; −1). | Analytical Geometry (Paper 2) |

*(Mapped to official CAPS topic list)*

| # | Question | Topic Tested |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | A 4 kg block is pulled up a 30° incline by a 40 N force parallel to the slope. The coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.2. Draw a free-body diagram and calculate the acceleration. | Mechanics — Newton's Laws |

| 7 | Name the organic product formed when ethanol reacts with ethanoic acid. Write the balanced equation and name the reaction type. | Organic Chemistry — Reactions |
| 8 | A source emits sound at 850 Hz and moves toward a stationary observer at 30 m·s⁻¹. If the speed of sound is 340 m·s⁻¹, calculate the frequency heard. | Waves — Doppler Effect |

## 3. Week-by-Week Study Plan

> **Plan assumptions:** 6 weeks to the November 2026 exams; ~5 study days/week; ~3–4 hours/day split between the two subjects. Alternate between Maths and Physical Sciences daily to prevent fatigue. Past-paper practice intensifies in the final two weeks.

| Week | Subject Focus (Official CAPS Topics) | Study Material | Past-Paper Work | Milestone / Check |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1** | **Diagnostic + Maths Algebra, Equations & Inequalities; Sequences & Series** | DBE Mind the Gap Maths (Algebra & Sequences chapters) · DBE Exam Guidelines Maths (education.gov.za) | Attempt 2024 Paper 1 Q1–Q2 only; mark with memo | Complete all 8 Maths diagnostics; identify ≥3 weak areas to flag |
| **Week 1** | **Physical Sciences: Mechanics — Newton's Laws, Free-Body Diagrams, Friction** | DBE Mind the Gap Physical Sciences Part 1 Physics (Mechanics chapter) · Stanmore Physics past papers (stanmorephysics.com) | Attempt 2024 Paper 1 Q2 (Newton's Laws); mark with memo | Complete all 8 Sciences diagnostics; flag weak areas |
| **Week 2** | **Maths: Functions & Graphs (Parabola, Hyperbola, Exponential, Logarithm, Inverses)** | DBE Mind the Gap Maths (Functions chapter) · Siyavula Grade 12 Maths (free at siyavula.com) | Attempt 2023 Paper 1 Q4–Q5 (Functions) full question | Draw and fully label 2 functions from scratch without notes |
| **Week 2** | **Physical Sciences: Mechanics — Projectile Motion, Work-Energy Theorem, Power** | DBE Mind the Gap Physical Sciences Part 1 (Work, Energy & Power chapter) · Stanmore Physics | Attempt 2023 Paper 1 Q3 (Projectile Motion) | Solve 3 projectile problems without a calculator hint |
| **Week 3** | **Maths: Differential Calculus (Rules of differentiation, turning points, optimisation, graph of f')** | DBE Mind the Gap Maths (Calculus chapter) · DBE Exam Guidelines (calculus elaboration) | Attempt 2022 Paper 1 Q8 (Calculus full question) | Correctly identify and classify all turning points of a cubic |
| **Week 3** | **Physical Sciences: Electricity — Ohm's Law, Series-Parallel Circuits, EMF, Internal Resistance** | DBE Mind the Gap Physical Sciences Part 1 (Electricity chapter) · Stanmore Physics | Attempt 2022 Paper 1 Q7–Q8 (Electricity) | Calculate terminal voltage and current in a mixed circuit correctly |
| **Week 4** | **Maths: Finance, Growth & Decay (simple, compound, effective rate, annuities, present value)** | DBE Mind the Gap Maths (Finance chapter) · DBE Examination Guidelines — Finance notes | Attempt 2023 Paper 1 Q5 (Finance) | Correctly calculate effective vs. nominal interest |
| **Week 4** | **Physical Sciences: Organic Chemistry (IUPAC naming, functional groups, reactions, polymers)** | DBE Mind the Gap Physical Sciences Part 2 Chemistry (Organic chapter) · SA Exam Papers (saexampapers.co.za) | Attempt 2023 Paper 2 Q4–Q5 (Organic) | Name 10 organic compounds correctly from structure |
| **Week 4** | **Maths: Probability (Venn diagrams, tree diagrams, counting principles)** | DBE Mind the Gap Maths (Probability chapter) · DBE Exam Guidelines | Attempt 2022 Paper 1 Q11 (Probability) | Complete a full Venn diagram + tree diagram question without errors |
| **Week 5** | **Maths Paper 2: Euclidean Geometry (theorems, riders, proofs — ALL 8 theorems)** | DBE Mind the Gap Maths (Geometry chapter) · DBE Exam Guidelines — Acceptable Reasons list (pp. 9–14) | Attempt 2024 Paper 2 Q10–Q11 (Geometry) | Write out 3 geometry proofs from memory with full reasons |
| **Week 5** | **Maths Paper 2: Trigonometry (general solutions, compound & double angles, graphs) + Analytical Geometry** | DBE Mind the Gap Maths (Trig chapter) · Siyavula Gr 12 Maths | Attempt 2023 Paper 2 Q5–Q6 (Trig) + Q7 (Analytical Geo) | Solve a compound-angle proof and a trig equation without notes |
| **Week 5** | **Physical Sciences: Chemical Equilibrium (Kc, Le Chatelier) + Acids & Bases (pH, titrations)** | DBE Mind the Gap Physical Sciences Part 2 Chemistry (Equilibrium & Acids) · Stanmore Physics | Attempt 2023 Paper 2 Q3–Q4 (Equilibrium + Acids) | Calculate Kc and predict equilibrium shift for 3 different disturbances |
| **Week 6** | **Full timed mock: Maths Paper 1 (3 hours) + Maths Paper 2 (3 hours)** | Use 2021 NSC Paper 1 & Paper 2 from testpapers.co.za or saexampapers.co.za — mark strictly with official memos | Full timed sittings — 2021 Paper 1 Monday, 2021 Paper 2 Wednesday | Score ≥60% on both papers; identify persistent errors for targeted revision |
| **Week 6** | **Full timed mock: Physical Sciences Paper 1 (3 hours) + Paper 2 (3 hours)** | Use 2021 NSC Phys Sci Paper 1 & Paper 2 from saexampapers.co.za or stanmorephysics.com | Full timed sittings — 2021 Paper 1 Tuesday, 2021 Paper 2 Thursday | Score ≥60% on both papers; review ALL incorrect calculations |
| **Week 6 (final days)** | **Targeted revision on lowest-scoring topics from mock exams + Formula sheet mastery** | DBE formula/information sheets (included in Exam Guidelines PDFs) · Mind the Gap revision summaries | Re-do only the question types you lost marks on in the mock | No new topics — consolidate and rest; review all formula sheets |

> **Daily rhythm suggestion:** 2 hours Maths + 2 hours Physical Sciences. Alternate heavier topics (Calculus, Mechanics) with lighter ones (Finance, Organic naming) on the same day to stay sharp.
