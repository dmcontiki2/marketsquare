🌿 Pretoria Outdoorsy Weekend — Two Friends · Budget under R1 000

Saturday

Time Plan Est. Cost (2 people) MarketSquare
06:30 Pretoria Boeremark (Farmers' Market), Silverton — A lively open-air farmers' market that takes place every Saturday morning in Silverton, Pretoria — with free entrance and a warm, family-friendly atmosphere. With over 200 stalls, the market offers fresh farm produce, homemade baked goods, handcrafted items, and classic SA treats like melkkos and vetkoek. Arrive early — stalls are freshest before 8:00. Grab breakfast here. Free entry + ~R80 breakfast for two (est.) —
09:00 Groenkloof Nature Reserve — Africa's first proclaimed game sanctuary (1895), right in the heart of Pretoria. Open 7 days a week, from 6 am to 6 pm (summer and winter). Adult entry is R51 pp. Card payments only — no cash accepted at the gate. The three hiking trails are the Red (1.3–3.5 km), White (3.7 km), and Yellow (10.5 km) trails. Do the White Trail (~1.5 hrs) for a great balance of wildlife and scenery. Lots of options for either a relaxed family hike or more adventurous — perfect to get everyone outdoors on weekends. R102 total entry (est.) —
12:00 Picnic lunch at Groenkloof — Grab supplies at the Boeremark earlier (bread, cheese, fruit). Game viewing is also possible from a 5 km motor-car route — do a slow drive through after your hike. ~R60 picnic supplies (bought at Boeremark) —
14:00 Faerie Glen Nature Reserve — A quick 20-minute drive east. Open 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM, with entry allowed until two hours before closing on weekends. Adult entry: R17 pp. Famous for hiking, dog walking, picnicking, and photography. Over 150 bird species found here. Three main loop trails: the Hadeda Trail (2.3 km, easy), Acacia Trail (3.2 km, moderate), and Kiepersol Trail (2 km, challenging). R34 total entry (est.) —
16:30 Sunset golden hour at Faerie Glen — Wrap up the hike and watch for zebras up close! Head back to base. R0 —
19:00 Dinner — Duncan Yard, Hatfield — Relaxed al fresco courtyard eatery area near UP. Budget ~R150–180 for two with drinks at a mid-range spot. ~R160 (est.) —

Saturday Sub-total (2 people): ~R436

Sunday

Time Plan Est. Cost (2 people) MarketSquare
08:00 Pretoria National Botanical Garden, Brummeria — A gorgeous 76-hectare oasis. Open Monday–Sunday: 08h00–18h00 (no entry after 17h00). Adult entry: R80 off-peak (April–August) / R100 peak (September–March). Home to over 220 bird species, small mammals like the slender mongoose and scrub hare, and more than 50 butterfly species. A paved nature trail gives access to the fascinating natural vegetation on the ridge. Enjoy a picnic at the Eziko Braai Area (charcoal only), or book a guided tour in advance. Arrive early for the birds. R160–200 total entry (est.) —
10:30 Coffee at Milkplum Café (inside the Botanical Garden) — Open daily, also serving as a conference and function venue. Grab a flat white and a bite while watching the garden fill with light. ~R80 (2 coffees + snacks, est.) —
12:30 Union Buildings Gardens — A 10-minute drive from the Botanical Gardens. There is no fee to enter the Union Buildings' grounds, which are open daily. The outdoor gardens are open to the public free of charge, with several hiking trails along the terraced gardens. Tour the terraced garden, featuring memorials to past presidents, and see the 30-foot statue of Nelson Mandela rising above the garden. The panoramic views from the hilltop are unmatched, and the beautifully landscaped gardens add to the peaceful atmosphere. Free —
14:00 Busstop7 Open Air Market — A Pretoria East institution, open on Sundays. Open Sundays 09:00–16:00. With over 150 stalls, discover hand-poured candles, organic veggies, raw honey, and freshly cut flowers. There's a quirky, inclusive vibe — sustainability is the buzzword. Live music, local brews, and picnic tables encourage visitors to linger. Browse, graze on street food, sip a local craft beer — the perfect Sunday wind-down. ~R80 street food & drinks (est.) —
16:30 Wrap-up — Sunset drive home R0 —

Sunday Sub-total (2 people): ~R360–400 (off-peak season) / ~R440 (peak season)
