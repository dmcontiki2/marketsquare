🌿 Pretoria Outdoorsy Weekend — Two Friends

Currency: ZAR (South African Rand) · Emergency: 10111 (police) / 10177 (ambulance) · Budget ceiling: R1,000 total for 2 people

Saturday

Time Plan MarketSquare
07:45 Hazel Food Market — Arrive early to beat the crowds. This lively outdoor market runs every Saturday 08:00–14:00 at PHSOB Hofmeyr Park, Queens Crescent, Lynnwood, featuring 70+ stalls with fresh produce, artisanal foods, and international cuisines ranging from Dutch and Indian to Greek and Italian. Admission is free. Allow 1.5–2 hrs. Budget for food/snacks: est. R80–R150 pp. → Hazel Food Market —
10:00 Pretoria National Botanical Garden — Walk the paved ridge trail, spot birds and dassies. A favourite for birdwatchers and nature lovers, this peaceful space is home to over 220 bird species, small mammals like the slender mongoose and common duiker, and more than 50 species of butterflies. Enjoy a picnic at the Eziko Braai Area (charcoal only) or book a guided tour in advance. Open Mon–Sun 08:00–18:00, no entry after 17:00. Entry: R70/adult (Apr–Aug off-peak) / R90/adult (Sep–Mar peak). Pack your own picnic from the market! Allow 2–2.5 hrs. → SANBI Pretoria Garden —
12:30 Picnic lunch inside the Gardens — Spread out on the lawns with your Hazel Market haul. There's a restaurant on site, plenty of clean restrooms, and drinking water available throughout the gardens. Free (already inside). —
14:30 Acrobranch Pretoria North — Treetop Adventure & Zipline — Burn off the braai rolls. Within a farm-side village on the northern side of Pretoria, expect a wild experience of slides, balance beams, jumps, and swings — many obstacles are high up in the trees. The park features the longest zipline in Gauteng at 300 m. Park closes at 14:00 on 24 & 31 Dec only; otherwise open weekends. ⚠️ Confirm session slots & prices directly — est. R250–R350 pp. Allow 2–3 hrs. → Acrobranch Pretoria North —
17:30 Sunset drinks at Hazeldean Brewing Co Taproom — Wind down on the farm. The Hazeldean Brewing Co Taproom is situated on Hazeldean Valley next to Hazeldean Valley Trails, where outdoor enthusiasts enjoy time out on the farm right on the doorstep of Pretoria East. Craft beers: est. R60–R80 pp. —

Sunday

Time Plan MarketSquare
06:00 Rietvlei Nature Reserve — Self-Drive Game Safari — The best wildlife hour. Gates open at 06:00 in winter (times may vary by season). Cheetah, black wildebeest, eland, common zebra, buffalo, hippo, and more all roam the reserve. Entrance fees are typically R68–R75 per adult; confirm the latest at the gate. Latest entry: 16:00; gates close at 18:00. Roads are generally well-maintained; most are tarred or easily navigable by a standard hatchback. Allow 3–4 hrs. Bring your own packed breakfast & coffee. 🐾 No 4x4 needed! → Rietvlei – City of Tshwane —
10:30 Picnic at Marais Dam Picnic Site inside Rietvlei — Braai and picnic spots are available at the dam, which also has a viewing deck. A bird hide overlooks the water at the Marais Dam Picnic Site. Braai hire: est. R50. —
13:00 Busstop7 Open Air Market (Pretoria East) — A relaxed post-safari browse. For a blend of countryside calm and urban creativity, Busstop7 Open Air Market opens Saturdays and Sundays 09:00–16:00, with over 150 stalls selling everything from hand-poured candles and organic veggies to raw honey and freshly cut flowers. Live music, local brews, and picnic tables encourage visitors to linger. Free entry. Budget for food/drinks: est. R80–R120 pp. → Busstop7 Facebook —
15:30 Austin Roberts Bird Sanctuary — Short, rewarding wind-down stroll. Named after the renowned ornithologist Austin Roberts, this sprawling 29-acre nature reserve is home to over 170 species of vibrant birds, including white-breasted cormorants and Egyptian geese, and offers an exhibition hall for visitors to explore. Entry: Free (small parking fee may apply, est. R10). Allow 1–1.5 hrs. —
17:00 Dinner in Hatfield / Brooklyn — Wrap up at any outdoor-patio restaurant of your choice in Hatfield or Brooklyn (est. R120–R180 pp for a meal + drinks). Popular options include areas along Burnett Street, Hatfield. —
