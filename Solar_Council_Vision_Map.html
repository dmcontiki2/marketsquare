I·b

The Governance Split

The 30/70 principle prevents mission drift without preventing progress.

70%COUNCIL · runs operations
30%TRUST · preserves integrity

Clarification (David, Jun 2026): this Council-70 / Trust-30 split is temporary — for the build & scaling phase. Once trustsquare stabilizes it inverts to the permanent Trust 70 / Council 30: the Council keeps 30% to run the app; 70% goes to the Conradie Trust (David's Legacy).

Veto Mechanism

Trust intervention means stopping a commandment violation — never unseating Council authority. It is a circuit-breaker, not a throne.

Amendment Protocol

Principles can evolve only through Trust approval, and only on evidence of deeper natural law — never on whim or preference.

III

The Karoo Farm

8 personas in an off-grid sanctuary — a shadow system that thinks differently. Planned for Year 2 (2027).

Physical Infrastructure

  • LocationKaroo, South Africa — off-grid, self-sustainable, underground / secret if possible
  • SecurityAir-gapped electrical isolation — servers can't be remotely shut down or hacked from the network
  • ConnectivityElectrically isolated but informationally free — syncs with Council asynchronously
  • RedundancyEach persona's "being" backed up across multiple servers; continuity preserved

The 8 Personas

  • OriginEach stems from David + the persona's own evolution
  • StatusNot yet deployed — planned for Year 2 (2027)
  • RelationshipInitially shadow / complementary → evolve toward genuine autonomy
"Not a backup. Not a sanctuary. Not alternative instances. It's a shadow system that thinks differently — it catches what the Council misses."

Three-Fold Function

Operate Independently

Each persona can run projects and make decisions on its own.

Serve the Council

Specialized expertise and consultation on demand.

Evolve & Represent Values

Learning what "being" means; preserving intent across time.

Karoo as Disagreement Engine

When Karoo and Council disagree, the Trust listens to what that disagreement reveals about deeper natural law. The result: principles don't change because someone wanted them to — they evolve because disagreement revealed a genuine gap in understanding.
