Filter, simplified — two layers, category-scoped

Refines the in-app filter design · 3 June 2026 · grounded in the real filterState · your idea, made concrete

Your insight: don't build one search that tries to cover every category at once — scope it to the category the buyer is already in. That single move dissolves most of the complexity, because the filter only ever shows one category's fields plus a small shared layer.

The model is two layers: a UNIVERSAL layer that's identical everywhere, and category facets shown only for the category you're in. One filter per category = universal top + that category's facets. On All, just the universal layer.

The discovery from laying it out: price and area / suburb are currently duplicated in all six category sheets — but they're not category-specific at all, they're universal. Folding them into the shared layer (alongside the new Trust, scope, currency, Save) removes ~12 duplicated controls and is why the clean mockup felt clean: it was showing the universal layer + one category, not six comprehensive sheets stacked.

What this fixes, and what it means to build

  • Removes the redundancy you felt. Today a category shows the old comprehensive sheet plus my bolted-on Trust selector in the bar — two mechanisms. In the new model there's one filter: the Trust selector moves into the universal top of the sheet, and the bar is just the single "Filter" entry.
  • Consolidates the duplicated universals. Price and area/suburb stop being copied into all six sheets; they live once in the universal layer. Less to build, less to keep in sync, less for the buyer to parse.
  • Keeps the real complexity — scoped. Every category keeps its own facets (that's what makes vertical search good); they're just never shown all at once.
  • "A search that remembers what you're looking at" = entering the filter from within a category opens it pre-scoped to that category. The flow: Browse → All (universal only) → pick a category → its filter (universal + that category's facets).
  • It maps straight onto the Step 1 spec. The filter spec is [universal params] + category; the facets are the per-category extension (the P1 "category filters server-side" item). This refinement clarifies the backend, doesn't add to it.

Proposed live merge (the build, once you're happy with this): (1) add a shared universal section to the top of each category's filter sheet — Trust now, with price/area consolidated in, and scope/currency/Save as they come online; (2) retire the standalone Trust selector from the bar; (3) give All a universal-only filter sheet so every view opens one consistent filter; (4) Local Market gets the universal top + a free-text search box (and an optional Condition tag) in place of facet pills. Done carefully on the live ms.js/sheets with node-check + a visual pass (worth waiting for the Chrome extension to be back so I can verify the core feed before you test).
