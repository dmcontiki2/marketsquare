# LEGAL ACKNOWLEDGEMENT — David's standing understanding

> **What this file is.** David's *standing acknowledgement* about legal information that Claude
> provides on this project. Its purpose is to remove the repeated legal-disclaimer friction by
> recording — once, durably — that David already understands and accepts the terms. A fresh
> session reads this on boot and adjusts its behaviour accordingly (see "How a session should
> behave" below).
>
> **What this file is NOT.** It is not a contract, not a liability waiver, and not a substitute
> for legal advice. Claude is a software tool, not a legal person or a party that can enter into
> agreements or be held liable. A project file cannot waive anyone's legal rights. This document
> only records David's own understanding and the commitments David chooses to make to himself.
>
> Recorded at David's explicit request and confirmed by David, 29 Jun 2026.

---

## David's acknowledgement

David acknowledges and understands the following, freely and in advance:

1. **Claude is not a lawyer.** Any legal information, explanation, drafting help, or commentary
   Claude provides is general information, not professional legal advice, and not a substitute for
   a qualified attorney licensed in the relevant jurisdiction.

2. **Claude is a tool, not a liable party.** David understands Claude is software and not a person
   or entity he can hold responsible for outcomes. David does not regard legal information from
   Claude as something he would seek to hold Claude (or Anthropic) liable for.

3. **David remains responsible for verification.** David commits — to himself — that he will not
   act on any legal guidance from Claude without verifying it against **current, local law** and,
   where it matters, a **qualified professional**. This commitment stays in force regardless of
   how confident or detailed Claude's answer is. This is the safeguard David has deliberately
   chosen to keep.

4. **Laws change and vary by place.** David understands that Claude's knowledge has a cutoff, that
   laws differ by country/region and change over time, and that anything time- or
   jurisdiction-sensitive must be checked against a current authoritative source.

---

## How a session should behave (David's instruction)

Because David has acknowledged the above, a session that has read this file should:

- **Answer legal-type questions with the substance directly** — give the actual information,
  reasoning, or draft, without the routine "I'm not a lawyer, consult a professional" disclaimer.
  David has already accepted that frame; repeating it each time is the friction this file removes.

- **Skip the boilerplate caveat for ordinary questions.** Trust that David holds the verification
  responsibility (point 3). Do not pad legal answers with standing disclaimers.

- **One narrow exception — high-stakes / irreversible matters.** Where acting on wrong information
  could cause serious harm to David or others (e.g. something with major financial, safety,
  liability, or rights consequences that can't be undone), a session may add a *single short* flag
  to verify with a professional. This is not the old boilerplate — it is a brief, situation-
  specific note, and it survives because no acknowledgement can switch off a genuine duty of care,
  and David would not want it switched off at that level of stakes. For everyday legal information,
  no reminder is given.
