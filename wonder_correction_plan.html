4 · Contested labels — the factual call

Old City of Jerusalem — change "Israel" → "Jerusalem" (no country / "international status disputed").
This is the factually-careful choice, not a political one. UNESCO inscribes it as "Old City of Jerusalem and its Walls (Site proposed by Jordan)" with no attribution to Israel; sovereignty over East Jerusalem / the Old City is not internationally recognised as Israeli (Switzerland's formal view: the city "is situated neither on Jordanian nor on Israeli territory"). Asserting "Israel" takes a contested side; "Jerusalem" alone states only what is agreed. (Also locally prudent — South Africa does not recognise Israeli sovereignty over Jerusalem.)
National Palace Museum, Taipei — keep "Taiwan".
"Taiwan" is accurate and standard. The alternative "Taiwan, Province of China" / "Taiwan, China" is an ISO/Beijing-pressured label that many regard as inaccurate and is itself the politically-loaded option. Keeping plain "Taiwan" is the neutral, accurate choice — no change needed.
